What it is
A Pi cluster that sits on your desk

Mark-Cluster is an open-source 3D print project — print the parts, slot in your Raspberry Pis, and you've got a tidy, stackable mini-datacenter for your home lab or office.

🖨️
Print-ready STLs
All parts exported and tested on FDM printers.
🔌
Cable management
Built-in channels keep USB and ethernet tidy.
🌬️
Airflow design
Vented sides and fan mount keep temps low.
📐
Modular bays
Add or remove Pi nodes without reprinting.

Getting started
Build your own

Four steps from zero to a running cluster on your desk.

01
Clone the repo
Run git clone https://github.com/you/mark-cluster to grab the STL files and config.
02
Print the parts
Open the STLs in your slicer. Recommended settings: 0.2mm layer height, 20% infill, PLA or PETG.
03
Gather components
Order everything listed in the Bill of Materials above. Most parts ship from the usual suppliers.
04
Assemble & flash
Follow the assembly guide in the repo README. Flash Raspberry Pi OS on each node and you're running.
